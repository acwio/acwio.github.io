I am a computer scientist that conducts interactive systems research at the intersection of human-computer interaction (HCI), computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW), and artificial intelligence (AI). My research focuses specifically understanding the role that interactive systems play in guiding human attention in every-day work and life.

I am currently an Applied Scientist at Amazon Science. I was previously a professor and co-founder of the People, Agents, Interactions, and Systems research group at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. Before that, I was a postdoctoral researcher in the the University of California, Irvine's Informatics Department. I earned my PhD from the University of Waterloo where I was advised by Edith Law and Edward Lank.

You can reach me at: acwio [at] amazon [dot] com.

Research Overview

My research focuses on designing novel intelligent and interactive systems and understanding how they affect or support human attention. My work is regularly published at premier publication venues for HCI research (e.g., CHI, CSCW, UIST) alongside other venues that welcome interdisciplinary contributions (e.g., applications of computing to the humanities).
